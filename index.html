  • Little Angels School, Sonipat has retrofitted its campus on international standards to keep its campus COVID free: Let’s take a look at its journey

    The ongoing COVID-19 crisis has created a paradigm shift in the field of education. In this hour of calamity, many educational institutions are trying to rise above the challenges through ground-breaking initiatives. In a move to not let the adverse circumstances hamper the education of students, Little Angels School, Sonipat has also rejigged its functioning and operations. Run by Angels Educational Society, a registered body, the institution was founded in 1993 by Late Dr. V.P. Arya, an educationist, visionary and an administrator par excellence. The institution has now completed 27 glorious years of Excellence. School Chairman Ashish Arya has inherited his father's passion and inclination towards the advancement of the Indian Education system. Educationally enlightened, technologically savvy and open to pedagogical changes, Arya, with the cumulative efforts of all the members under the aegis of the school, have taken the institution to dazzling heights. The School Director, Dr. Neha Chandra Arya, who has a background in medical sciences, has also provided her valuable guidance and advice in the implementation of safety measures necessary for students and staff in the post-pandemic world. In the last few months, immediate steps were taken under the able guidance of the school’s Principal, Asha Goyal, to deploy digital learning. The school is now putting in place every safety measure possible to resume physical classes as per the Government’s order. It is also implementing a slew of steps to keep the campus safe from Covid-19 as it’s time to gear up to resume physical classes soon.

  • Schoolchildren join fight against noise

    Mumbai: Students of Trinity International, Sion (E), and Little Angels High School, King Circle, participated in Times of India’s ‘No Honking Drive’ initiative in collaboration with Awaaz foundation and Times Newspaper in Education (NIE). The sessions were curated by Sumaira Abdulali, founder of Awaaz Foundation, and aimed at educating students about the effects of sound pollution.
